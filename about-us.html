When you solve a puzzle



When you solve a puzzle, your brain is actively engaged in problem-solving and decision-making. This process involves several areas of the brain, including the prefrontal cortex, which is responsible for planning and decision-making, and the temporal lobes, which are involved in memory and perception. As you work on a puzzle, your brain is constantly processing and integrating information from your senses, as well as recalling and using stored information from your memory. This process of actively thinking and problem-solving can be mentally stimulating and can help to keep your brain active and healthy. Solving puzzles can also activate the release of neurotransmitters, such as dopamine and serotonin, which are associated with feelings of pleasure and satisfaction. This can contribute to the feeling of accomplishment and enjoyment that many people experience when they solve a puzzle.



Excess Screen Time Affect



There are a number of potential problems that children and adolescents may face as a result of excessive screen time. These can include physical problems such as eyestrain, neck and back pain, and carpal tunnel syndrome, as well as more psychological issues such as sleep disturbances, difficulty with social interactions, and problems with attention and concentration. Children who spend a lot of time in front of screens may also be at risk for poor mental health outcomes, such as anxiety, depression, and other mental health disorders. Additionally, children who spend a lot of time on screens may be at risk for developing unhealthy habits such as snacking while using screens, sedentary behavior, and a lack of physical activity.



What is STEAM



STEAM toys are educational toys that are designed to engage children in activities that promote learning in the fields of science, technology, engineering, arts, and mathematics (STEM/STEAM). These toys often take the form of puzzles or games that require children to use critical thinking and problem-solving skills to complete. Puzzle STEAM toys may involve building, constructing, or creating something, and can help children develop skills such as spatial awareness, fine motor skills, and problem-solving abilities. They may also expose children to concepts in STEM/STEAM fields and can help spark an interest in these subjects at an early age. Many puzzle STEAM toys are designed to be hands-on and interactive, making them an engaging and fun way for children to learn.



How We Select a Puzzle



Level of difficulty: The difficulty level of a puzzle can be an important factor in determining its appeal and replay value. A puzzle that is too easy may not be satisfying to solve, while a puzzle that is too difficult may be frustrating. Originality and creativity: A puzzle that is unique and creative can be more engaging and enjoyable to solve. Clarity and fairness: A puzzle should be clear and fair, with all of the necessary information provided and no hidden tricks or unfair elements. Quality of materials: The quality of the materials used in a puzzle, such as the paper, ink, or plastic, can affect the overall experience of solving the puzzle. Length of time to solve: The length of time it takes to solve a puzzle can be a factor in its enjoyment, as a puzzle that is too short may not be satisfying, while a puzzle that is too long may be frustrating. Replay value: A puzzle that can be solved multiple times without losing its appeal can be more satisfying to solve. Thematic appeal: A puzzle with a theme that is interesting or appealing to the solver can enhance the enjoyment of the puzzle. Ease of storage: A puzzle that is easy to store and transport can be more convenient to work on and can encourage more frequent solving.
